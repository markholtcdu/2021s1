    • Driverless cars

  • Self-driving cars already exist and are gradually being used in people's lives in today's world. The World Health Organization points out that 1.2 million people die in traffic accidents each year (WHO, 2015). Driverless cars are considered to an important technology to reduce partial deaths caused by human error (Kyriakidis et al., 2015). In the author's (Kanwal & Giselle) article, they cited (Benenson et al., 2008; Godoy et al., 2015). The viewpoint stated that most people believe that driverless cars will not become a global leader in the near of future. Mainstream on most roads and according to Miralles & Domene (2010) mentioned the most likely adoption settings may be in closed environments such as university campuses, airports, golf courses, holiday parks and retirement villages.
  • Self-driving cars on the roads of the future It is an inevitable trend that the self-driving car will still need to be verified in future practice. According to the author Tao et al (2017) and others, it remains a major challenge when using automotive sensors such as single-frequency Global Navigation Satellite System (GNSS) receivers, a lane detection camera, and proprioceptive sensors. The technology is still immature and still requires a lot of time and investment to benefit people’s lives.
